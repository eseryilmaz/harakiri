# Harakiri Decision Input Template

Copy this template and fill in the details before invoking the skill.
The more specific you are, the more specific and useful the attack will be.

---

```
Harakiri this:

Decision:
[Describe the exact decision in one or two sentences. Be specific.
Pricing changes need numbers. Feature removals need what is being removed.
Rebrands need the before and after. Campaigns need the actual copy.]

Sector:
[SaaS · Consumer app · Fintech · E-commerce · Media / creator · Agency · Marketplace · Other]

Primary audience affected:
[Who feels this most directly? Existing customers · New customers · Free users ·
Enterprise buyers · Developers · Creators · General public]

Announcement framing (optional):
[How do you plan to present this? Copy the intended announcement language if you have it.]

Context the market will use against you (optional):
[Past promises. Competitor pricing. Previous price increases. Vocal customer segments.
Internal skeptics. Relevant industry context. Anything that could be weaponized.]
```

---

## Examples of good vs weak input

### Weak input (produces generic output)

```
Harakiri this: we are changing our pricing.
```

### Strong input (produces specific, useful output)

```
Harakiri this:

Decision: We are increasing pricing from $29/month to $49/month across all plans,
effective in 30 days. Existing customers are automatically moved to the new price.
There is no grandfather period and no legacy pricing option.

Sector: SaaS / productivity

Primary audience affected: Existing paying customers, mostly indie hackers and
small teams. Average customer tenure is 14 months.

Announcement framing: "We are investing heavily in the product and this pricing
reflects the value [Product] delivers today."

Context: We raised prices once before, 18 months ago ($19 → $29). A vocal
community exists on Reddit. Competitor X is at $35/month with more features.
We have not shipped a major feature in 6 months.
```

---

## Notes

- You do not need to fill every field. Decision + sector + audience is enough to get started.
- The optional context fields are where the most dangerous attacks come from. Fill them if you can.
- If you have the actual announcement copy, paste it. The skill attacks the framing, not just the decision.
