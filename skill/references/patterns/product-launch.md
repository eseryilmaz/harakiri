# Product Launch — Calibration Patterns

This file contains synthetic complaint patterns for tone calibration. The examples are illustrative, not real customer quotes. Do not reproduce them verbatim in generated reports.

---

## Pattern 1: Overpromise / Underdeliver

**Emotional trigger:** Disappointment sharpening into cynicism. The gap between the demo and the real product.

**Complaint angle:** The launch presentation set expectations the product cannot meet at release. The gap is not a quality complaint — it is a trust complaint. The company knew what the demo showed and shipped something different.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "The demo was not the product. The product is not the demo. This is always how it works."
- "Six months of hype for a beta that can't do the thing they showed on stage."
- "Every launch video lies. I will wait for the third-party reviews."

**Likely platforms:** Hacker News, X, Reddit, YouTube comments

**Who amplifies it:** Technical users who test products rigorously; reviewers and newsletter writers who compare launch claims to actual capability

**What reduces the anger:** Accurate demo footage showing the real product in real conditions; a clear distinction between what's in the launch and what's on the roadmap

---

## Pattern 2: AI Feature Fatigue

**Emotional trigger:** Exhaustion and distrust. Poorly integrated AI features are read as filler.

**Complaint angle:** The AI feature does not solve a real problem better than the existing solution. It adds steps, adds risk, and produces lower-quality output than the thing it replaced — while being presented as innovation.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "AI slop dressed up as a product update."
- "They added AI to justify the price increase. The feature doesn't work."
- "Nobody asked for an AI to do this. We asked for them to fix the bug we reported two years ago."

**Likely platforms:** Hacker News, X, Reddit, developer communities

**Who amplifies it:** Developers and technical users who can articulate specifically why the AI implementation is poor; anyone tired of AI being used as a marketing term

**What reduces the anger:** Showing specific before/after comparisons with real user workflows; making AI features opt-in rather than default

---

## Pattern 3: Competitor Latency

**Emotional trigger:** Dismissiveness. The launch is too little, too late.

**Complaint angle:** The market has already decided. Competitors solved this problem years ago and have stronger implementations. This is a features catch-up play, not a genuine innovation.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "The competitor has had this for two years. And done it better."
- "This is a checkbox feature built for the press release, not for the user."
- "They shipped to say they shipped it. Not because it's ready."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Hacker News

**Who amplifies it:** Users of competing products; analysts and writers who cover the category; anyone who evaluated both products and chose the competitor

**What reduces the anger:** Demonstrating specific ways the implementation improves on the competitor's version; or being honest that this is v1 of a longer roadmap

---

## Pattern 4: Existing User Neglect

**Emotional trigger:** Resentment. Long-term users feel ignored while resources go to attracting new users.

**Complaint angle:** The launch is targeting new users or new use cases. Meanwhile, outstanding bugs, long-requested features, and degraded performance for existing users remain unaddressed.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "New shiny feature. Same bugs I reported eighteen months ago."
- "They're building for the pitch deck, not for the people actually using it."
- "I don't need a new AI thing. I need the core product to work reliably."

**Likely platforms:** Reddit, community forums, app store reviews

**Who amplifies it:** Power users with long tenure and high engagement; anyone who has submitted support tickets or feature requests that were not addressed

**What reduces the anger:** Pairing the launch with a visible fix to a high-vote existing issue; acknowledging outstanding user requests in the launch post

---

## Pattern 5: Hype Cycle Skepticism

**Emotional trigger:** Wariness. Users who have been disappointed before are not willing to engage.

**Complaint angle:** The launch follows a familiar pattern of announcement, hype, and gradual deterioration. This audience is not angry — they are simply not participating. Their silence and skepticism are the threat.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "I'll believe it when I see it in production for six months."
- "They've announced this before with different names. We'll check back in a year."
- "Hard pass until there are reviews from people who aren't affiliated with the company."

**Likely platforms:** Hacker News, Reddit, industry newsletters

**Who amplifies it:** Experienced users who have been through previous launch cycles; journalists who cover the space and remember prior announcements

**What reduces the anger:** Shipping something that works without caveats; inviting independent reviewers before the launch rather than after
