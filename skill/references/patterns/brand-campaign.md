# Brand Campaign — Calibration Patterns

This file contains synthetic complaint patterns for tone calibration. The examples are illustrative, not real customer quotes. Do not reproduce them verbatim in generated reports.

---

## Pattern 1: Authenticity Gap

**Emotional trigger:** Hypocrisy recognition. The campaign claims values that the company's actions contradict.

**Complaint angle:** The campaign says one thing; the product, pricing, or support record says another. Users who have experienced the gap between the stated values and the actual behavior will treat the campaign as evidence of dishonesty rather than aspiration.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Great marketing. Too bad the product doesn't back it up."
- "They say customer-first. My three-week support ticket says otherwise."
- "The campaign is for people who don't use the product yet."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Reddit, review sites

**Who amplifies it:** Existing users who feel the campaign misrepresents their experience; journalists who cover brand credibility; anyone with a negative recent interaction

**What reduces the anger:** Aligning the campaign message with a concrete product or policy change that demonstrates the value; not running values-forward campaigns while known issues go unresolved

---

## Pattern 2: Mission Drift

**Emotional trigger:** Disillusionment. Early adopters feel the company has changed and the campaign confirms it.

**Complaint angle:** The campaign signals a repositioning that betrays the original community. The brand that users chose for one reason is now presenting itself as something else, and the campaign makes the drift visible.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Remember when this company was about [original value]? This campaign proves they've forgotten."
- "The pivot is visible. This is not the brand I signed up for."
- "They used to be the scrappy alternative. Now they're doing the same thing as everyone else."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, community forums, newsletters

**Who amplifies it:** Early adopters and original community members; anyone who chose the product because of what it used to stand for

**What reduces the anger:** Explicitly acknowledging the original mission and explaining the evolution; giving the original community early access or recognition

---

## Pattern 3: Unprovable Claim

**Emotional trigger:** Skepticism and mild contempt. The campaign makes a superlative or exclusive claim that cannot be verified.

**Complaint angle:** "The only," "the best," "the fastest" — claims without evidence invite scrutiny and ridicule. When competitors can immediately demonstrate the claim is false, or when no evidence is offered, the campaign becomes a target.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "The only tool that does X? I can name three others. Did anyone fact-check this?"
- "Bold claim. Where's the data?"
- "Every company says they're the only one. None of them are."

**Likely platforms:** X, Hacker News, LinkedIn

**Who amplifies it:** Competitors' users and advocates; journalists and analysts who cover the category; anyone with enough category knowledge to disprove the claim

**What reduces the anger:** Replace superlative claims with specific, verifiable differentiators; "we do X differently because Y" is defensible; "the only" is not

---

## Pattern 4: Out-of-Touch Creative

**Emotional trigger:** Secondhand embarrassment turning into distrust of brand judgment.

**Complaint angle:** The campaign creative — the tone, the imagery, the copy — signals that the team making it does not understand or use the product. It reads as designed for a different audience, or designed purely for an award.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "This ad was made by committee and it shows."
- "Designed for the screenshot, not for the user."
- "Who is this for? It's not for anyone I know who uses this product."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Reddit, creative industry communities

**Who amplifies it:** Existing users who feel misrepresented; creative professionals who can articulate exactly why the work fails; anyone with strong taste who finds it embarrassing

**What reduces the anger:** User-generated content and real customer language in creative; testing campaign concepts with actual users before production

---

## Pattern 5: Virtue Signal Without Substance

**Emotional trigger:** Cynicism. The campaign aligns with a cause or value without changing any behavior.

**Complaint angle:** The campaign makes a values statement — about community, sustainability, privacy, fairness — that is not backed by any specific commitment or policy change. Users treat it as PR rather than principle.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Great campaign. Where's the action?"
- "Tweeting about privacy while the settings page makes it a twelve-step process to opt out."
- "This is what saying the right thing instead of doing the right thing looks like."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Reddit

**Who amplifies it:** Advocates for the cause being invoked; anyone who can point to a specific inconsistency between the campaign and the company's actual practices

**What reduces the anger:** Pair the campaign with a specific policy change or commitment; do not invoke values that the product actively undermines

---

## Pattern 6: Credibility Gap

**Emotional trigger:** Skepticism shading into dismissal. An unknown or unproven product is promoted with authoritative claims and no supporting evidence.

**Complaint angle:** The campaign makes a strong positioning claim — "setting the new standard," "the only tool that," "faster and better than" — for a product the audience has never heard of or tested independently. The gap between the confidence of the claim and the absence of evidence reads as promotional content dressed as editorial judgment.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Bold claim. Zero evidence. Classic agency content."
- "Never heard of this tool. No benchmark cited. But sure, it's 'setting the new standard.'"
- "This reads as paid promotion. Is it paid promotion?"

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, Hacker News, marketing communities on Reddit

**Who amplifies it:** Industry professionals who know the category and can immediately identify the absence of evidence; journalists covering undisclosed paid content; anyone who has reviewed competing tools independently

**What reduces the anger:** One verifiable data point (a benchmark, a speed test, a third-party review) changes the entire credibility profile; disclose any commercial relationship with the product being promoted

---

## Pattern 7: Expertise Mismatch

**Emotional trigger:** Secondhand embarrassment combined with loss of trust in the source.

**Complaint angle:** The content quality — the copy, the creative, the argument structure — contradicts the poster's positioning as a professional authority. An agency account with generic copy, a consultant with a factual error, a thought leader with a meme that backfires. The audience does not just reject the content; they revise their opinion of the source.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "This is the account I recommended to clients for marketing advice."
- "The caption contradicts the image. Did anyone proofread this?"
- "A marketing agency posted this. About marketing. Let that land."

**Likely platforms:** LinkedIn, X, marketing and design communities

**Who amplifies it:** Industry peers who follow the account for professional content; clients or potential clients of the posting account; anyone who has cited or recommended the account publicly

**What reduces the anger:** Content quality review before posting that specifically checks whether the copy matches the creative, whether claims are verifiable, and whether the overall execution reflects the account's stated level of expertise
