# Policy Change — Calibration Patterns

This file contains synthetic complaint patterns for tone calibration. The examples are illustrative, not real customer quotes. Do not reproduce them verbatim in generated reports.

---

## Pattern 1: Trust Withdrawal

**Emotional trigger:** Betrayal at a foundational level. The relationship with the company has changed.

**Complaint angle:** The policy change signals that the implicit contract between the company and its users has been rewritten. Users who committed to the platform based on prior terms now feel the ground shifted under them.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Read the new terms. This is not the same company I signed up with."
- "The policy is: whatever is legal for us, regardless of what is right for you."
- "They changed the rules after I was already locked in. That is not a partnership."

**Likely platforms:** Reddit, X, Hacker News

**Who amplifies it:** Long-tenure users who feel a sense of relationship with the product; anyone who made a significant commitment (paid, migrated data, built workflows) under the prior terms

**What reduces the anger:** A genuine comment period before the change goes into effect; grandfathering users who signed up under materially different terms; a clear explanation of what changed and why

## Pattern 2: Data Exploitation Narrative

**Emotional trigger:** Violation and distrust. The policy change is read as monetizing what users thought was private.

**Complaint angle:** The policy update permits the company to use user content, behavior, or data in ways that were not previously disclosed. Users frame this not as a business decision but as a betrayal of the implicit agreement that their data was theirs.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Read section 4.2. Your content is now their training data."
- "The product was always going to be free until they figured out how to monetize what you put into it."
- "When the privacy policy changes, ask yourself: what did they need permission to do that they couldn't do before?"

**Likely platforms:** Hacker News, Reddit (r/privacy), X

**Who amplifies it:** Privacy advocates, security researchers, journalists who cover data practices, anyone who reads terms of service carefully

**What reduces the anger:** Explicit opt-in for any new data use; a clear plain-language summary of what changed; third-party privacy audit results if available

## Pattern 3: Retroactive Application

**Emotional trigger:** Outrage at the unfairness of applying new rules to past behavior.

**Complaint angle:** The policy change applies to content created or actions taken before the new terms existed. Users feel they are being judged or monetized under rules they never agreed to.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "This applies to content I created before they changed the rules. That is not okay."
- "Retroactive policy changes are the tech industry's way of stealing from users legally."
- "Everything I posted is now subject to terms I never agreed to at the time."

**Likely platforms:** X, Reddit, creator communities, legal commentary spaces

**Who amplifies it:** Legal observers; creators who have published significant content on the platform; privacy advocates; journalists covering platform power

**What reduces the anger:** Applying the new policy only to content created after the effective date; providing a way for users to delete or export prior content before the change goes live

---

## Pattern 4: Consent Theater

**Emotional trigger:** Manipulation. The policy change technically offers opt-out, but the default and the process make real consent impossible.

**Complaint angle:** The change was implemented with an opt-out buried in settings, pre-checked boxes, or a notification that most users would not open. The appearance of consent was maintained while real consent was bypassed.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Opt-out is in Settings → Privacy → Advanced → Manage Preferences. For a change that affects everyone."
- "They sent a notification that looked like a feature update. The policy change was in paragraph four."
- "If you need to hide it in the settings to get compliance, you already know it's not something people want."

**Likely platforms:** X, Hacker News, Reddit, privacy communities

**Who amplifies it:** Privacy advocates who can walk through the opt-out process publicly; journalists who test the UX of consent flows; anyone who finds the buried opt-out and shares it

**What reduces the anger:** Opt-in rather than opt-out for any material change; a clear, prominent, single-action consent mechanism; no pre-checked boxes

---

## Pattern 5: Creator and Power User Exodus Signal

**Emotional trigger:** Alarm and fear of platform decline.

**Complaint angle:** A visible or vocal user announces they are leaving because of the policy change. Their departure is read as a signal, not just a personal decision. Their audience follows the story and the platform's health becomes a question.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "When the people who built their livelihood here start leaving, the platform is over."
- "This policy change was designed to extract value from creators while pretending to support them."
- "The ecosystem depends on people they just alienated. Good luck with that."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, creator newsletters, YouTube

**Who amplifies it:** Anyone with an audience in the relevant community; journalists who cover the platform; competing platforms who benefit from the exodus

**What reduces the anger:** Direct outreach to affected power users before the policy goes public; a carve-out or exception for high-engagement users; visible evidence that creator feedback shaped the final policy
