# Pricing Change — Calibration Patterns

This file contains synthetic complaint patterns for tone calibration. The examples are illustrative, not real customer quotes. Do not reproduce them verbatim in generated reports.

---

## Pattern 1: Loyalty Penalty

**Emotional trigger:** Betrayal. Long-tenure customers feel punished for staying.

**Complaint angle:** The decision is framed as a tax on loyalty — the people who committed earliest and stayed longest are now paying more than anyone who joins after the new price.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Early adopters always get burned. We built the product for you, now we pay for it."
- "I've been here since before they were profitable. This is the thank you."
- "They got us dependent, then changed the price once leaving felt painful."

**Likely platforms:** Reddit, X, app store reviews

**Who amplifies it:** Long-tenure users, community leaders, anyone who publicly recommended the product

**What reduces the anger:** A grandfather period for existing customers at the prior rate; acknowledgment of the prior commitment in the announcement

---

## Pattern 2: Enshittification Arc

**Emotional trigger:** Cynicism. The price increase is framed as the latest step in a predictable value-extraction sequence.

**Complaint angle:** Users recognize the pattern — make it good to build dependency, then extract once leaving is costly. The price increase is not a surprise; it is the confirmation of a suspicion they already had.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Another company speedrunning the enshittification playbook."
- "Free → cheap → expensive → unusable. We're at step three."
- "Screenshot this. In two years it will be twice this price and half the product."

**Likely platforms:** X, Hacker News, Reddit

**Who amplifies it:** Tech-savvy commenters, newsletter writers covering SaaS trends, anyone who has seen this before

**What reduces the anger:** Difficult to address once the framing is established. Pre-empt by committing publicly to a price ceiling or a clear rationale tied to specific investments.

---

## Pattern 3: Value Regression

**Emotional trigger:** Frustration. Users feel the product has declined in quality while the price has increased.

**Complaint angle:** The price increase is being asked for while visible quality indicators — support response time, bug fix cadence, feature delivery — have gone in the opposite direction.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "The product has gotten slower and the price has gotten higher. Impressive."
- "What exactly are we paying more for? I have three open bug reports from six months ago."
- "They charge premium prices for a product that's gotten worse every quarter."

**Likely platforms:** Reddit, Trustpilot, G2, app store reviews

**Who amplifies it:** Power users with long tenure who track product quality closely

**What reduces the anger:** Specific product commitments tied to the pricing rationale; a public roadmap item that visibly addresses outstanding complaints

---

## Pattern 4: Competitor Arbitrage

**Emotional trigger:** Validation for switching; decision made easier for fence-sitters.

**Complaint angle:** The competitor's price is cited as a direct argument. This persona is not angry — they are relieved to have the proof they needed.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "Moved to [alternative] six months ago at half this price. Glad I did."
- "Competitor does this for less and doesn't pull this. Easy choice."
- "They just made the decision for everyone who was on the fence."

**Likely platforms:** X, LinkedIn, comparison review sites

**Who amplifies it:** Former customers, evaluation-stage prospects, anyone who was already considering alternatives

**What reduces the anger:** Hard to address directly. The comparison only disappears if the price delta closes or the value gap widens visibly.

---

## Pattern 5: Compound Increase

**Emotional trigger:** Pattern recognition. A second or third increase triggers a qualitatively different response than a first.

**Complaint angle:** The current increase is not evaluated in isolation — it is the latest data point in a documented trend. Users who tolerated the first increase now feel that tolerance was a mistake.

**Synthetic calibration phrases:**
- "First increase I understood. Second one I accepted. This one I'm done."
- "They raised prices eighteen months ago. Now again. What's the next one?"
- "I gave them the benefit of the doubt twice. Not a third time."

**Likely platforms:** Reddit, X, support forums

**Who amplifies it:** Users who stayed through previous increases and now feel their patience was not rewarded

**What reduces the anger:** Explicit acknowledgment of the previous increase in the announcement; a stated commitment on cadence or ceiling
